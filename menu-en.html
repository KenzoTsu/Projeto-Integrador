Problems

Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Mechanics, Astronomy.

Malba Tahan, in "Arithmetic, Algebra, Geometry, Mechanics, Astronomy," emphasizes the interconnection of mathematical disciplines and their relevance for understanding the world, addressing everything from simple calculations to complex geometric equations.

The Four Fours.

In the tale "The Four Fours" from "O Homem que Calculava," Malba Tahan presents a mathematical challenge where one must express the number 1 using exactly four digits "4" and various mathematical operations.

Pearls of the Rajah.

In "Pearls of the Rajah," Malba Tahan analyzes the value of pearls, which includes stories and cultural meanings. The work connects geography, economics, and symbolism, using mathematics to illustrate trade exchanges, highlighting pearls as symbols of cultural wealth and exchange.

Lighter Pearl.

In the problem "Lighter Pearl," Malba Tahan explores the use of mathematics to calculate the weight and density of pearls. The challenge involves subtle comparisons, revealing the beauty of mathematical applications in natural phenomena.

About the book

The Man Who Counted.

The narrative, set against the backdrop of the medieval Islamic world, follows the mathematical adventures of the protagonist, who extraordinarily solves and explains various mathematical problems, puzzles, and curiosities. It also includes legends and picturesque stories, such as the legend of the origin of chess and the story of the philosopher and mathematician Hypatia of Alexandria. While it is not a textbook, it has a strong moral tone. Therefore, the book is recommended as a supplementary reading in several countries, having been mentioned in the magazine Book Report and various publications of the genre.
